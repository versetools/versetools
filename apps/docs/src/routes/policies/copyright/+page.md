## Notification of Copyright Infringement Claims

Making original work is hard! As described in our [Use Restrictions policy](./abuse), you can’t use {config.name} products\* to make or disseminate work that uses the intellectual property of others beyond the bounds of [fair use](https://www.gov.uk/guidance/exceptions-to-copyright).

Are you a copyright owner? Under the [UK Copyright, Designs and Patents Act 1988 (“CDPA”)](https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/1988/48/contents), you have the right to notify us ({config.company.name} trading as {config.name}) if you believe that an account user of any product we built and maintain has infringed on your work(s) as copyright owner. To be effective, the notice of claimed infringement must be written. Please include the following information:

- A physical or electronic signature of you, or of a person authorised to act on behalf of the copyright owner.
- Identification of the copyrighted work(s) claimed to have been infringed. If there are multiple works, please provide a representative list.
- Sufficient information to help us locate the material you believe infringes the copyrighted work.
- Your name and contact details so that we can respond to you. An email address is preferred, but a telephone number or postal address is also acceptable.
- A statement that you believe, in good faith, that the use of the material complained of is unauthorised by the copyright owner, their agent, or the law.
- A statement that the information in your notice is accurate and that you are authorised to act on behalf of the copyright owner.

## Infringement Counter-claims

On the flip-side, if you believe your material has been removed in error, you can file a written counter-claim. Please include the following information:

- A physical or electronic signature, or the signature of the person authorized to act on your behalf.
- A description of the material that was removed.
- A description of where the material appeared in {config.name} products prior to their removal.
- Your name and contact information so that we can get back to you. Email address is preferred but a telephone number or mailing address works too.
- A statement that you request restoration of the material and that you consent to us sharing your counter-notice with the person who submitted the original complaint, where required or appropriate.
- A statement that you acknowledge the matter may be referred to the courts of England and Wales, Scotland, or Northern Ireland, as applicable, if the dispute is not resolved.
